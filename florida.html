Florida Notary Journal Requirements

The split every Florida notary needs to know

← Back to Notary Journal

Current as of 2026-07-01

The short answer

It depends on how you notarize. If you perform traditional, in-person notarizations, Florida does not require you to keep a journal. If you are commissioned as an online notary public and perform remote online notarization (RON), you must keep an electronic journal under Florida Statutes section 117.245.

This split trips people up constantly. A lot of circulating "which states require a journal" lists just mark Florida as "required" or "not required" with no distinction, and both answers are half wrong.

If you only notarize in person

Florida does not mandate a journal for traditional notarization. Many Florida notaries keep one anyway, since it is standard defensive practice if a notarization is ever questioned in a dispute or a fraud claim, and some employers or title companies require it as a matter of internal policy even though the state does not.

If you perform remote online notarization

Online notaries public must keep an electronic journal of every RON transaction. Under section 117.245, the entry set includes the date and type of the act, the type of document, the signer's name, how you identified the signer, and the fee charged, including a "no fee" entry when you waived it. The electronic journal is retained for 10 years.
